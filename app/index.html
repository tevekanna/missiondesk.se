Steps 2–7 – iGRC · GRC · ARC · SAIL
// SORA 2.5 QUANTITATIVE RISK ASSESSMENT · JARUS JAR-doc-25 TABLE 2
▸ STEP 2 – INTRINSIC GROUND RISK CLASS (iGRC)
SORA 2.5 determines iGRC from characteristic dimension (m) and max speed (m/s) + population density. Dimensions fetched automatically from DOI step. iGRC scales 1–10; values >7 are outside SORA scope (certification category).
▸ STEP 3 – GROUND RISK MITIGATIONS (FINAL GRC) – ANNEX B
SORA 2.5 categorises mitigations as M1A (sheltering), M1B (operational restrictions), M1C (ground monitoring) and M2 (technical energy reduction on impact).
▸ STEP 4 – INITIAL AIR RISK CLASS (ARC) — DECISION TREE
Initial ARC is determined from the Airspace Encounter Class (AEC) decision tree [SORA v2.5 Main Body, Figure 6 and Annex C, Table C.1]. Answer each question in order.
▸ STEP 5 – STRATEGIC MITIGATION (RESIDUAL ARC)
The operator may demonstrate that the local manned traffic density in the operational volume is lower than the generalised AEC rating, reducing the ARC. [SORA v2.5 Annex C, Table C.2]  |  Note: AEC 10, 11 and 12 are not in Table C.2.
▸ STEP 6 – TACTICAL MITIGATION PERFORMANCE REQUIREMENT (TMPR)
TMPR is determined automatically from the residual ARC. VLOS/EVLOS operations are exempt from TMPR — see-and-avoid is an acceptable tactical mitigation for all ARC levels. [SORA v2.5, Section 4.6.4]

Step 8 – Containment
// NEW DEDICATED STEP IN SORA 2.5 · ANNEX E §E.4
Containment ensures that the Target Level of Safety is achieved in the adjacent area. In SORA 2.5, containment is a dedicated step (#8) before OSO. Three robustness levels: Low / Medium / High. Requirements depend on UA size, SAIL, population density in adjacent area, and presence of crowds within 1 km.
